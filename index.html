Self-learning player in Splendor

Splendor is a strategic board game, which can be played by two or four players, in this project, we will focus on two players and the rule of Splendor is whoever reaches 15 score first wins the game, if both players reach 15 at the same round, then the player that has the less card on hand wins the game. There are three strategies we used when designing, which are Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS), Reinforcement Learning(Q-learning), and Minimax Tree.

Feature Extraction in Facial Recognition

In this project, we investigate how does the choice of feature extraction techniques affect the robustness of three different models on face verification. To investigate this, this research aims to examine the effect of the choice of feature extraction techniques on the performance of models including traditional model like Support Vector Machine(SVM), deep learning model of Support Vector Machine combines with Convolution Neural Network (SVM-CNN), and Hybrid Vision Transformer (ViT) as modem transformers as in the context of face verification and compare each model’s performance on original data with aligned face preprocessing and distorted data with occlusion and noise.
